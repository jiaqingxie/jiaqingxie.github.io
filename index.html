Jiaqing Xie

AI for science, molecular representation learning, and scientific agents.

I am a CS PhD candidate at Fudan University. My research focuses on machine learning for chemistry and physical science, especially spectroscopy-grounded molecular modeling, scientific foundation models, and agentic systems for research and discovery.

I am interested in building models, benchmarks, and systems that help machine learning interact more naturally with molecular structure, spectra, and scientific workflows. My recent work spans NMR and vibrational spectroscopy, structure-aware molecular learning, and agent frameworks for chemistry.

Research Themes

Three directions that define my current research agenda

Spectroscopy as a machine learning interface

Build models and benchmarks that connect NMR and vibrational spectra with molecular understanding, generation, and structure elucidation.

NMRGym NMRTrans SpecMol

Structure-aware foundation models for molecules

Push molecular representation learning beyond text-only or graph-only views by injecting geometric and structural inductive bias into model design.

SA-Attn GraphPEBench DeepProtein

Agentic systems for chemistry and research

Design modular agent frameworks and evaluation settings that let language models reason, act, and collaborate on scientific workflows.
